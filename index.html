Born in Singapore, raised in Mumbai, and trained across Boston, New York, Geneva, and Florida — Yahn Kizilbash is a performer whose world is as wide as his ambition. At the American School of Bombay, he completes his IB Diploma with HL subjects in Music, English, and Sports Science.

His voice has been trained by Daniel Singh using Speech Level Singing Technique since 2018, and by Stephen Fernandes at SOBO House of Music since 2012. In summer 2025, he attended Berklee College of Music's Vocal Summit and Songwriting Workshop in Boston — performing an original co-written song and earning the Berklee Online Scholarship Award for leadership and enthusiasm.

He studies theatre and writing privately with Prof. Mark Olsen of the Juilliard School since 2020, completed Columbia University's Pre-College Program in 2024, and has three summers of Juilliard Performing Arts in Florida. He is also a certified Stanford PACS Young Philanthropist.

Running parallel to all of this: over six years of hands-on philanthropy at St. Catherine of Siena orphanage in Bandra, a platform launching in 2026, and a conviction that art and service are not separate lives — they are the same one.
